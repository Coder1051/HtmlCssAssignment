Early Life and Education

Imran Khan was born on October 5, 1952, in Lahore, Pakistan, into a prominent Pashtun family. He was the only son among four sisters. Imran's early education was at Aitchison College in Lahore, after which he attended the Royal Grammar School in Worcester, England. He pursued higher education at the prestigious University of Oxford, where he earned a degree in Philosophy, Politics, and Economics (PPE) in 1975.

Cricket Career

Imran Khan’s cricketing journey began in the early 1970s, and he soon became Pakistan's most celebrated cricketer. Known for his fast bowling, all-round skills, and charismatic leadership, he led Pakistan's national team to numerous victories. His crowning achievement came in 1992 when he captained Pakistan to its first-ever Cricket World Cup victory. This moment not only elevated Imran to the status of a national hero but also established his legacy in the cricketing world. Imran’s playing style was marked by fierce competitiveness and a never-say-die attitude. He was widely respected for his strategic acumen and ability to motivate his team. His influence extended beyond cricket, as he was often seen as a symbol of national pride and resilience.

Philanthropy and Social Work

After retiring from cricket, Imran Khan turned his focus towards philanthropy. In 1994, he established the Shaukat Khanum Memorial Cancer Hospital & Research Centre in Lahore, named after his mother who had died of cancer. The hospital provides free cancer treatment to thousands of patients annually, particularly those who cannot afford medical care. In 2008, Imran founded Namal College, an institution aimed at providing quality higher education to students from rural areas. His philanthropic endeavors earned him widespread admiration and solidified his reputation as a man dedicated to improving the lives of ordinary Pakistanis.
